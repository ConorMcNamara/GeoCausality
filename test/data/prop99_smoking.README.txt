California Proposition 99 ("smoking") dataset
=============================================

File: prop99_smoking.csv  (long format: state, year, cigsale, + predictors)

Source
------
Abadie, A., Diamond, A., & Hainmueller, J. (2010). "Synthetic Control Methods
for Comparative Case Studies: Estimating the Effect of California's Tobacco
Control Program." Journal of the American Statistical Association, 105(490),
493-505.

This is the `smoking` dataset distributed with the R `Synth` package (Abadie,
Diamond & Hainmueller) -- the canonical Proposition 99 panel and one of the most
widely redistributed teaching datasets in the synthetic-control literature. The
vendored CSV is a faithful export whose values match the package exactly
(California cigarette sales: 123.0 packs in 1970, 90.1 in 1988, 41.6 in 2000).

What is vendored
----------------
39 states observed annually 1970-2000 (31 years each, balanced panel):

    state     state name; treated unit = "California"
    year      1970-2000 (integer)
    cigsale   per-capita cigarette sales in packs (the outcome `y`)
    lnincome, beer, age15to24, retprice
              the original Abadie predictors, kept for provenance; the
              GeoCausality estimators use only `cigsale`

California passed Proposition 99 in 1988 (a large tobacco-control program), so
it is treated from 1989 onward. The other 38 states form the donor pool.

Published result this fixture reproduces
----------------------------------------
Abadie, Diamond & Hainmueller (2010) estimate that by 2000 annual per-capita
cigarette sales in California were about 26 packs lower than the synthetic
control, with an average post-period (1989-2000) gap of roughly -19 to -20
packs. Our `SyntheticControl` recovers ~-19.5 (average) / ~-26.6 (year 2000).
