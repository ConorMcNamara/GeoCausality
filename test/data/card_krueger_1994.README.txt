Card & Krueger (1994) NJ/PA minimum-wage fast-food dataset
==========================================================

File: card_krueger_1994.csv  (long format: geo, date, y, state)

Source
------
Card, D., & Krueger, A. B. (1994). "Minimum Wages and Employment: A Case Study
of the Fast-Food Industry in New Jersey and Pennsylvania." American Economic
Review, 84(4), 772-793.

Underlying microdata: David Card's public data archive,
https://davidcard.berkeley.edu/data_sets/njmin.zip (file `public.dat`, 410
restaurants surveyed in two waves). The authors released this dataset publicly
for replication; it is one of the most widely redistributed teaching datasets in
applied econometrics.

What is vendored
----------------
`public.dat` was reshaped into one row per store-wave with a non-missing
full-time-equivalent employment value. Columns:

    geo    store id ("s<SHEET>" from the codebook)
    date   1992-02-01 (wave 1, Feb/Mar 1992, before the NJ wage rise) or
           1992-11-01 (wave 2, Nov/Dec 1992, after)
    y      FTE employment = EMPFT + NMGRS + 0.5 * EMPPT
           (full-timers + managers + half of part-timers), the paper's
           definition
    state  "NJ" (treated) or "PA" (control)

NJ raised its minimum wage from $4.25 to $5.05 on 1 April 1992; PA did not, so
PA serves as the control group.

Published results reproduced by this fixture (Table 3, FTE employment)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
    NJ: 20.44 (before) -> 21.03 (after)
    PA: 23.33 (before) -> 21.17 (after)
    Difference-in-differences = (21.03 - 20.44) - (21.17 - 23.33) = +2.76 FTE
